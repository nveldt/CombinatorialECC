Data obtained from https://www.datafiles.samhsa.gov/study-series/drug-abuse-warning-network-dawn-nid13516.

A hyperedge corresponds to the set of drugs a patient reported in a visit to the ER. The color is the most common patient disposition (where the patient went after the visit)
for that drug combination. The disposition labels are:
1: DISCHARGED HOME
2: RELEASED TO POLICE/JAIL
3: REFERRED TO DETOX/TREATMENT
4: ICU/CRITICAL CARE
5: SURGERY
6: CHEMICAL DEPENDENCY/DETOX, PSYCHIATRIC UNIT
7: OTHER INPATIENT UNIT
8: TRANSFERRED
9: LEFT AGAINST MEDICAL ADVICE
10: DIED
